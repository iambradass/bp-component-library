The Complete Digital Presence Framework for Title Reps

A professional digital presence for a title rep is exactly five things. Not six, not twelve — five. And they must all be done correctly, because partial implementation produces near-zero results. Build all five and you will start generating inbound leads. Skip any one and you'll wonder why it isn't working.

  • 01
    Your Domain — Independent of Any Employer
    Register yourname.com or yournametitle.com. This is the foundation everything else sits on. If you use a company domain, you lose it when you change companies. This one is yours forever.
    How to do it: Go to Namecheap.com. Search "lisaabramstitle.com" (or your variation). Buy the .com for ~$12/year. Don't let the registrar upsell you on email hosting, SSL, or any add-ons — you'll get those through your host.
    Cost: ~$12/year · Time: 15 minutes
  • 02
    Hosting + Professional Website
    Your website needs to be fast, mobile-first, and structured to rank on Google. A Squarespace or Wix site can technically work, but they are harder to rank and harder to maintain correctly. WordPress with a quality theme is the professional standard for local search.
    How to do it: Sign up for Hostinger or SiteGround hosting ($3–$5/month). Install WordPress. Buy the Astra Pro theme ($50). Install the Yoast SEO plugin (free). Build 5 pages: Home, About, Services, Service Areas, Contact. Write 400+ words on each page. Use your city name throughout naturally — not awkwardly stuffed.
    Cost: $50–$100/year + 40–60 hours of your time
  • 03
    Google Business Profile — The Most Important Step
    This is where most reps either skip entirely or do incorrectly. Your Google Business Profile is how you appear on Google Maps and in local search results. This single setup drives more inbound leads than anything else in this framework.
    How to do it: Go to business.google.com. Create a profile with your name + "Title Representative" as the business name. Set category to "Insurance Agency" (closest match for title). Add service areas — every city you work in. Write a 750-character description with your city names and what you do. Add 10+ photos. Request verification via postcard. Once verified: update your Q&A section, enable messaging, respond to every review. Ask 5 existing clients for reviews immediately after setup.
    Cost: Free · Time: 4–6 hours to set up correctly
  • 04
    Local SEO Basics
    You don't need to become an SEO expert. You need to do four things: create a page for each service area, get your name/address/phone (NAP) consistent across the web, get listed in local directories, and ensure your site loads quickly.
    How to do it: Create one page per city you serve (e.g., "Title Rep Fort Worth"). Each page should be 500+ words with local landmarks and specific service details. Submit your business to: Yelp, BBB, Bing Places, Apple Maps, and your local Chamber of Commerce. Use GTmetrix.com to test your site speed — aim for under 3 seconds. Install a caching plugin (WP Rocket, $49/year) if it's slow.
    Cost: $0–$100 · Time: 10–20 hours initial + ongoing
  • 05
    Lead Capture + CRM Follow-Up
    Traffic without lead capture is a leaking bucket. Every person who visits your site and doesn't contact you is a lost opportunity. You need a form that works, goes somewhere, and triggers a follow-up within minutes — not days.
    How to do it: Install WPForms Lite (free) or Gravity Forms ($59/year) on your WordPress site. Create a contact form: Name, Phone, Email, City/ZIP, "What can I help with?" field. Connect it to a free HubSpot CRM account. Set up a 3-email welcome sequence in HubSpot that goes out immediately, at 24 hours, and at 3 days. Every form submission gets a personal reply from you within 2 hours.
    Cost: $0–$60/year · Time: 8–12 hours to set up
