Founder Story

I Was the Title Rep Nobody Could Find Online.
So I Fixed It — Then Realized Every Rep Had the Same Problem.

Brad Patterson  ·  Founder  ·  March 2026  ·  8 min read

It was a Tuesday in 2019. I'd been a title rep for four years, had a solid book of business, and genuinely loved the work. But I had this nagging feeling that I was leaving opportunity on the table — not from lack of hustle, but from something I couldn't quite name.

A colleague — a newer rep with half my experience — closed 22 more transactions than me that year. When I finally asked him how, he pulled out his phone and showed me his website. Clean, professional, came up first when you searched his name and our city. He said, "Agents Google me before they call."

I Googled myself that night. Nothing came up except an old LinkedIn profile and a company page that barely mentioned my name. I had been invisible the whole time.

Who I Was Before I Built This

I wasn't a developer. I wasn't a marketer. I was a title rep who knew how to work a closing table, build relationships, and keep transactions moving when they got complicated. I was good at the job. I just had no idea how to market myself.

I tried making a website on Wix once. Spent a weekend on it, got frustrated with the templates, published something that looked embarrassing, and quietly forgot about it. I tried posting on LinkedIn more consistently. Got some likes, no leads. I asked my company's marketing team for help. They gave me a one-pager with the company logo on it.

None of it moved the needle. And I didn't know why.

The Problem I Couldn't Stop Thinking About

Once I built my own site and started getting leads, I couldn't stop noticing how many reps were exactly where I had been. Talented, hardworking, good at their jobs — and completely invisible online.

I started doing research. Pulled up 50 title rep websites across Texas. Of those, 31 didn't have their own domain — they were just pages on their company's site. 11 hadn't been updated in over two years. 6 had broken links or missing contact forms. Only 2 showed up on page one of Google for their city.

"The problem wasn't that reps didn't care about their digital presence. It was that nobody had shown them exactly how to build one — or offered to just do it for them."

September 14, 2023 — Fort Worth, TX

Had coffee with Marcus today. He's been in title for 9 years. Genuinely one of the best closers I know — agents love working with him because he never lets a deal fall apart. But when I searched his name after we said goodbye, nothing. Not a single useful result.

I kept thinking: Marcus is losing business right now, and he doesn't even know it. Someone is searching for a title rep in his city today, and a rep with half his experience is going to get that call because they have a website and he doesn't.

I have to figure out how to help people like him. This can't just be something I did for myself.

The Breakthrough Moment

After helping Marcus and two other reps, I realized something important: the process I had figured out was repeatable. The same elements worked every time — a well-structured personal domain, SEO-optimized copy written in plain language, a Google Business Profile configured correctly, a lead capture system that actually converted, and content that told their story credibly.

None of the individual pieces were complicated. The breakthrough was understanding how they fit together, in what order, and what actually moved the needle versus what just looked good.

And once I had that, I could build it for anyone in about three weeks.

"The best referral you'll ever get is from Google. It never forgets to mention your name."
