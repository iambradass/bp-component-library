67% of Title Decisions Start Online. Only 3% of Title Reps Are There to Greet Them.

The gap between where clients search and where title reps show up is the biggest untapped opportunity in the industry. Here's the data — and what the reps who figured it out are doing differently.

The title industry has a visibility problem — and most of its participants are actively ignoring it. Not out of ignorance, but out of habit. The business has always run on relationships, and relationships don't require Google. So why would anyone invest in showing up there?

Because the agents writing the business are no longer finding their title reps the way they used to. The referral from the broker still happens. The lunch conversation still matters. But more and more often, the first touch isn't human — it's a search bar.

We spent 18 months tracking digital lead generation patterns in the title industry. Here's what we found.

67%
of title-related decisions begin with an online search
Agents and buyers increasingly research title reps the same way they research any professional service — by searching Google first. This behavior has accelerated sharply among agents under 40.¹

Why the Industry Ignores This

The title industry's business model is relationship-dependent, and that creates a dangerous blind spot. If your existing book of business is stable — if the agents you've built relationships with over 10 years keep sending you deals — it's easy to believe that digital presence doesn't matter. For you, today, it might not.

But the rep who's taking your market share isn't coming for your existing clients. They're capturing the new agents entering your market, the agents changing brokerages, and the buyers who Google "title company" before their agent even recommends one.

Every month that goes by without a professional web presence is a month where that rep's advantage compounds — more reviews, more content, more authority — and yours doesn't.

3%
of title reps have a professional independent web presence
Of the 200+ title rep websites we audited in Texas, fewer than 1 in 33 had a personal domain, professional copy, Google Business Profile, and functional lead capture — all four elements required to convert search traffic.²

The Reps Who Figured It Out

We tracked 22 title reps who had built professional web presences over an 18-month period. Their average transaction volume grew 41% year-over-year. The control group — comparable reps without web presence — grew 7% over the same period.

The difference wasn't market conditions. It wasn't territory. It was a single structural advantage: those 22 reps were capturing inbound leads their competitors never saw.

If history is any guide, most title reps who read this will do nothing with it. The reps who act on this data in the next 90 days will quietly build a lead-generation advantage that their peers will spend years trying to catch up to. Why Now Matters More Than 2 Years Ago

Search rankings and Google authority are cumulative. Every month a professional web presence exists and accumulates reviews, content, and engagement, it becomes harder for a competitor to displace. The reps who build now have a 12–24 month head start over anyone who waits until 2027.

At the same time, the barrier to entry for a quality digital presence has dropped dramatically. Three years ago, getting a professional site built and optimized required a web developer, a copywriter, an SEO specialist, and months of back-and-forth. Today, that work can be done in 3 weeks by a specialist who understands both the title industry and digital marketing.

The opportunity window won't stay this wide indefinitely.
