Your Company's Website / Profile Page
Probably still your first hit when you Google yourself
We get it — it's there, it was free, and it's better than nothing. But that page exists to market the company, not you. Your name is listed alongside 10–20 other reps. There's no lead capture tied to you specifically. If you leave that company tomorrow, the page disappears with you.

Facebook Business Page
Posted consistently for 3 weeks, then it got quiet
Facebook doesn't rank you on Google for local searches. It's a social platform — it surfaces content to people who already follow you. If a new agent in your city searches "title rep Fort Worth," your Facebook page won't appear. You're speaking to an existing audience that's already seen your work, not capturing new ones.
Invisible to search · Requires constant content to maintain

Social Media Management Service
Paid $300/mo, got generic posts that could be for a dentist
Generic social media doesn't build search presence or credibility. If the posts could have been written for any professional service — and they probably could have — they're not doing the specific work a title rep needs: establishing expertise, ranking on Google, capturing leads from people who are actively searching.
Builds engagement, not searchability · Zero lead capture

DIY Wix or Squarespace Site
Started it, never finished, or finished and embarrassed by it
DIY website tools are genuinely fine — for someone who has time to learn design, copywriting, and local SEO. For a title rep whose time is spent closing transactions, the result is usually: half-finished, poorly designed, with no SEO, no copy strategy, and not connected to any lead system. Looking amateur is worse than not being there at all.
No SEO · Generic design · Hours of your time · Usually unpublished

Nephew / Friend / Cheap Freelancer
Built something technically live — but you don't want to share the link
We've heard this one more times than we can count. The site is live, yes. No copywriting expertise · No SEO strategy · Hard to maintain
