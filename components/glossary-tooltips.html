Understanding SEO in 2024

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) is the practice of improving a website's visibility in organic search results. Unlike PPC advertising, organic traffic doesn't stop when you stop paying. Done well, SEO compounds over time — pages built today can generate leads for years.

Technical Foundations

Every SEO strategy starts with a solid technical foundation. Core Web Vitals — Google's page experience signals — measure real-world performance using LCP, FID, and CLS. A high bounce rate is often the first sign of a technical or relevance problem. Crawlability — whether Google's bots can access and understand your pages — must be verified before any on-page work.

On-Page Optimization

Each page should target one primary keyword with natural supporting semantic keywords. The title tag and meta description are your ad copy in the SERP — write them for humans, not algorithms. Schema markup adds structured data that unlocks rich results, including FAQ snippets and star ratings.

Authority Building

Domain authority grows through earning quality backlinks. A single link from a high-authority site (like a major news outlet or university) is worth more than hundreds of low-quality links. Anchor text — the clickable words in a link — tells Google what the linked page is about. Avoid over-optimized exact-match anchors; natural variation looks more trustworthy.
